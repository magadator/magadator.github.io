About Me

I am a computational engineer specializing in high-fidelity simulations of complex flows. My research focuses on pulsatile turbulent flow in curved geometries inspired by the human aorta, with applications to cardiovascular disease modeling — building on an earlier foundation in high-speed aerospace CFD and fluid-structure interaction.

Research Highlights

Where Simulation Meets the Clinic and the Wind Tunnel

Aortic Flow DNS

Direct numerical simulation of pulsatile turbulent flow in curved pipes, isolating the roles of curvature and pulsation on Dean-vortex breakdown.

Aortic Dissection CFD

Patient-specific simulations using CT/MRI-reconstructed geometries, validated directly against 4D-flow MRI velocity fields.

ML Surrogates

Neural-network models for fast prediction of wall shear stress fields, accelerating CFD parameter studies.
