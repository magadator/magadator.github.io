About

From Aerospace to the Aorta

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Kentucky, where I specialized in direct numerical simulation (DNS) of pulsatile turbulent flow in curved geometries relevant to cardiovascular hemodynamics. My path started in high-speed aerospace CFD — nozzle flows, wing aerodynamics, and supersonic fluid-structure interaction — before turning toward patient-specific biomedical flows, and more recently toward machine-learning surrogates that make high-fidelity simulation more tractable.
